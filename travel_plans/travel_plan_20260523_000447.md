Here is a suggested 7-day Japan itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival in Tokyo

* Arrive at Tokyo Narita or Haneda airport
* Check-in to your hotel and freshen up
* Visit the famous Shibuya Crossing and take a walk around the Shibuya area
* Try some delicious Japanese street food for dinner

Day 2: Tokyo

* Visit the Tokyo Tower for panoramic views of the city
* Explore the Asakusa district, including the Senso-ji Temple
* Try some traditional Japanese cuisine for lunch
* Visit the Meiji Shrine, a serene Shinto shrine located in a tranquil forested area

Day 3: Tokyo to Kyoto

* Take a bullet train (Shinkansen) to Kyoto
* Check-in to your hotel and freshen up
* Visit the Fushimi Inari Shrine, famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates
* Explore the Gion district, known for its geisha culture

Day 4: Kyoto

* Visit the Kinkaku-ji Temple (Golden Pavilion)
* Explore the Arashiyama bamboo forest
* Try some traditional Japanese sweets and tea
* Visit the Nijo Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Day 5: Kyoto to Osaka

* Take a train to Osaka
* Check-in to your hotel and freshen up
* Visit the Osaka Castle, a majestic castle that played a crucial role in Japan's unification
* Explore the Dotonbori area, known for its neon lights and street food

Day 6: Osaka

* Visit the Universal Studios Japan theme park
* Try some delicious Osaka cuisine, including takoyaki and okonomiyaki
* Explore the Shinsekai district, a retro-style neighborhood

Day 7: Departure from Osaka

* Spend the morning shopping for last-minute souvenirs or exploring a local market
* Depart from Osaka Kansai International Airport

**Travel Tips:**

* Japan is a cash-based society, so make sure to have enough local currency (Japanese yen) with you.
* Learn some basic Japanese phrases, such as "konnichiwa" (hello), "arigatou" (thank you), and "sumimasen" (excuse me).
* Try to avoid traveling during peak season (June to August) to save on accommodation and transportation costs.
* Consider purchasing a Japan Rail Pass for unlimited train travel.
* Don't tip at restaurants or bars, as it's not customary in Japan.
* Respect Japanese culture and traditions, especially when visiting temples or shrines.
